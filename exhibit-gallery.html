EXHIBIT 01

Malolos Constitution

Assertion of Philippine Sovereignty

The Malolos Congress, convened on September 15, 1898, marked a pivotal moment in Philippine history. It brought together Filipino leaders from across the archipelago to deliberate and enact laws for the newly declared Philippine Republic. The adoption of the Malolos Constitution on January 21, 1899, was a bold assertion of Philippine sovereignty and independence from Spanish colonial rule. This constitution laid the foundation for the First Philippine Republic, embodying the aspirations of the Filipino people for self-governance and freedom from foreign domination.

EXHIBIT 02

Malolos Constitution

Pioneering Democratic Principles

The Malolos Constitution was a pioneering document that introduced democratic principles of governance to the Philippines. It established a republican form of government with a separation of powers among the executive, legislative, and judicial branches. The constitution enshrined fundamental rights and freedoms, including the right to liberty, equality, and due process of law. By promoting democratic ideals, the Malolos Congress and Constitution set a precedent for future Philippine constitutions and contributed to the development of democratic institutions in the country.

Leaders of the First Philippine Republic

Visionary Leadership and Sacrifice

The leaders of the First Philippine Republic were visionary individual who played crucial roles in the struggle Philippine independence. Emilio Aguinaldo, the first President of the Republic emerged as a symbol of Filipino resistance against Spanish and American colonial rule. His leadership during the Philippine Revolution and the Philippine-American War inspired Filipinos to fight for freedom and self-determination. Other notable leaders such as Apolinario Mabini, known as the "Brains of the Revolution", and Antonio Luna, a brilliant military strategist, made significant contributions to the cause of independence despite facing immense challenges and sacrifices. By learning about these leaders, visitors can insights into their courage, determination, and unwavering commitment to the Filipino nation.

EXHIBIT 01

Battles and Triumphs

Courage and Resilience in Battle

The Battles and Triumphs of the FIrst Philippine Republic highlight the courage and resilience of Filipino soldiers in the face of overwhelming odds. From the Battle of Tirad Pass, where a small Filipino force led by General Gregorio del Pilar bravely defended against American troops, to the Siege of Baler, where Filipino insurgents held out against Spanish forces for nearly a year, these battles exemplify the indomitable spirit of the Filipino people in their fight for freedom and independence. Through exhibits and historical artifacts, visitors can gain a deeper understanding of the sacrifices made by Filipino soldiers and their enduring legacy in Philippine history.

EXHIBIT 02

Battles and Triumphs

Strategic Victories and Tactial Brilliance

The Battles and Triumphs of the First Philippine Republic also showcase the strategic victories and tactical brilliance of Filipino military leaders. From the successful capture of Spanish garrisons in Cavite during the early stages of the revolution to the guerrilla warfare tactics employed by Filipino insurgents against American forces during the Philippine-American War, these battles demonstrate the ingenuity and adaptability of Filipino commanders in challenging colonial powers. By highlighting these triumphs, the exhibit gallery honors the strategic acumen of Filipino leaders and their contributions to Philippine military history.

EXHIBIT 01

Cultural Renaissance

Cultural Identity and Heritage Preservation

The Cultural Renaissance of the First Philippine Republic witnessed a flourishing of literary and artistic creativity, with Filipino writers and artists producing works that reflected the spirit of the times. Writers such as Jose Rizal, Marcelo del Pilar, and Graciano Lopez Jaena penned influential essays and literary masterpieces that challenged colonial hegemony and championed Filipino nationalism. Similarly, artists like Juan Luna and Felix Resurreccion Hidalgo gained international acclaim for their paintings, which depicted scenes from Philippine history and daily life.
