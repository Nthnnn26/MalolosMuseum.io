EXHIBIT GALLERIES

EXHIBIT 01

Malolos Constitution

Assertion of Philippine Sovereignty

The Malolos Congress, convened on September 15, 1898, marked a pivotal moment in Philippine history. It brought together Filipino leaders from across the archipelago to deliberate and enact laws for the newly declared Philippine Republic. The adoption of the Malolos Constitution on January 21, 1899, was a bold assertion of Philippine sovereignty and independence from Spanish colonial rule. This constitution laid the foundation for the First Philippine Republic, embodying the aspirations of the Filipino people for self-governance and freedom from foreign domination.

EXHIBIT 02

Malolos Constitution

Pioneering Democratic Principles

The Malolos Constitution was a pioneering document that introduced democratic principles of governance to the Philippines. It established a republican form of government with a separation of powers among the executive, legislative, and judicial branches. The constitution enshrined fundamental rights and freedoms, including the right to liberty, equality, and due process of law. By promoting democratic ideals, the Malolos Congress and Constitution set a precedent for future Philippine constitutions and contributed to the development of democratic institutions in the country.

EXHIBIT 03

Malolos Constitution

Cultural and Historical Significance

The Malolos Congress and Constitution hold immense cultural and historical significance in the Philippines. They represent symbols of Filipino resilience, unity, and the struggle for independence. The events at Malolos underscore the determination of Filipino leaders to establish a sovereign nation-state and govern themselves according to democratic principles. The Malolos Congress and Constitution are integral parts of Philippine heritage, embodying the spirit of nationalism and patriotism. By learning about these events, visitors to the exhibit gallery gain insights into the rich tapestry of Philippine history and the enduring legacy of Filipino heroes who fought for freedom and self-determination.
